# MultiTaction Canvus Custom Menu Tasks

This document provides detailed descriptions of all available tasks in the custom menu.

## Task 1: SWOT Analysis

**Description:** A collaborative SWOT analysis framework that helps teams identify and analyze Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats related to business competition or project planning.

**Expected Outcome:** A complete SWOT analysis with color-coded notes for each quadrant (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats).

**Implementation Details:**
- Central title note with SWOT Analysis heading
- 2x2 grid of semi-transparent notes for each quadrant
- Color-coded note stacks in each corner:
  * Green notes (top-left): Strengths
  * Red notes (top-right): Weaknesses
  * Blue notes (bottom-left): Opportunities
  * Purple notes (bottom-right): Threats

**Use Case:** Strategic planning sessions, business analysis, project kickoffs, and team brainstorming.

## Task 2: Business Model Canvas

**Description:** A strategic management template that helps teams visualize and develop their business model in a structured way, covering key aspects of a business.

**Expected Outcome:** A completed business model canvas with notes for each of the 9 key components of a business model.

**Implementation Details:**
- Title note with Business Model Canvas
- 9 sections organized in a specific layout:
  * Key Partners
  * Key Activities
  * Key Resources
  * Value Propositions
  * Customer Relationships
  * Channels
  * Customer Segments
  * Cost Structure
  * Revenue Streams

**Use Case:** Business planning, startup development, business model innovation, and strategic pivots.

## Task 3: Stakeholder Mapping

**Description:** A visual representation of the various stakeholders involved in a project, organized by their level of interest and influence.

**Expected Outcome:** A stakeholder map with positioned stakeholders based on their interest and influence levels, with appropriate engagement strategies for each quadrant.

**Implementation Details:**
- Interest/Influence grid with four quadrants
- Color-coded notes for different types of stakeholders
- Defined engagement strategies for each quadrant
- Visual indicators of stakeholder relationships

**Use Case:** Project planning, change management, communication planning, and stakeholder engagement.

## Task 4: Process Mapping

**Description:** A visual representation of a business process or workflow, showing the sequence of steps, decision points, and relationships between different activities.

**Expected Outcome:** A clear process map with workflow steps, decision points, and connections showing the flow from start to finish.

**Implementation Details:**
- Start and end points clearly marked
- Process steps represented as rectangles
- Decision points represented as diamonds
- Arrows showing the flow direction
- Color-coding for different types of activities or departments

**Use Case:** Process improvement, workflow optimization, training, and documentation.

## Task 5: Risk Assessment Matrix

**Description:** A tool for evaluating and prioritizing risks based on their likelihood of occurrence and potential impact.

**Expected Outcome:** A 5x5 risk assessment matrix with color-coded zones and notes for identified risks.

**Implementation Details:**
- 5x5 grid with axes for likelihood and impact
- Color-coded zones (green, yellow, orange, red)
- Notes for each identified risk, positioned according to likelihood and impact
- Clear labels for different risk levels

**Use Case:** Project risk management, business continuity planning, safety assessments, and strategic decision-making.

## Task 6: Prioritization Matrix

**Description:** A decision-making tool that helps teams evaluate and rank items based on defined criteria such as impact and effort.

**Expected Outcome:** A prioritization matrix with items mapped based on their evaluation against selected criteria.

**Implementation Details:**
- 2x2 grid with axes for criteria (typically impact vs. effort)
- Color-coded quadrants with clear labels
- Items positioned according to their evaluation
- Clear guidance for each quadrant

**Use Case:** Feature prioritization, project selection, resource allocation, and strategic planning.

## Task 7: Brainstorming/Ideation

**Description:** A collaborative space for generating and organizing ideas around a central topic or challenge.

**Expected Outcome:** A collection of organized ideas with grouping, categorization, and initial evaluation.

**Implementation Details:**
- Central challenge or topic note
- Color-coded categories or themes
- Space for idea generation
- Voting or ranking mechanism
- Action planning section

**Use Case:** Innovation workshops, problem-solving sessions, creative thinking, and team collaboration.

## Task 8: Decision Matrix

**Description:** A systematic approach to evaluating options against multiple criteria, with weighted scoring to support objective decision-making.

**Expected Outcome:** A decision matrix with options as rows and criteria as columns, with scores for each option against each criterion.

**Implementation Details:**
- Notes arranged in a grid format
- Options listed as rows
- Criteria listed as columns
- Scores entered in the grid cells
- Weighted calculations for final evaluation

**Use Case:** Vendor selection, hiring decisions, product comparisons, and multi-criteria decision-making.

## Task 9: Project Timeline

**Description:** A visual representation of a project schedule showing phases, milestones, activities, and deadlines over time.

**Expected Outcome:** A visual project timeline with key milestones, activities, and deadlines clearly displayed chronologically.

**Implementation Details:**
- Horizontal timeline with notes for each phase/milestone
- Color-coded notes for different types of activities
- Clear indication of dependencies and critical path
- 2% boundary from viewport edges
- No overlapping notes for clarity

**Use Case:** Project planning, status reporting, schedule management, and team coordination.

## Task 10: Meeting Notes Template

**Description:** A structured template for capturing and organizing meeting discussions, decisions, and action items.

**Expected Outcome:** A structured template for capturing meeting notes with predefined sections for agenda, attendees, decisions, and actions.

**Implementation Details:**
- Title note with meeting details (MT Purple color)
- Color-coded sections for different parts of the meeting:
  * Meeting information and attendees (MT Blue)
  * Agenda items (MT Dark Blue)
  * Discussion notes (MT Purple)
  * Decisions made (MT Magenta)
  * Action items with assignees and due dates (MT Blue)
  * Next steps (MT Dark Blue)
- 2% boundary from viewport edges
- No overlapping notes for clarity
- Efficient space usage with appropriate note sizes

**Use Case:** Regular team meetings, project reviews, client meetings, and workshop facilitation.

## Task 11: Retrospective Template

**Description:** A structured template for conducting team retrospectives to review past work, identify lessons learned, and plan improvements.

**Expected Outcome:** A structured template for conducting team retrospectives with sections for what went well, what needs improvement, and action items.

**Implementation Details:**
- Title note with retrospective details (MT Dark Blue)
- Sprint information section at the top (MT Blue)
- Color-coded sections for different feedback categories:
  * What went well (MT Blue)
  * What needs improvement (MT Magenta)
  * New ideas (MT Purple)
- Action items with owners and due dates (MT Dark Blue)
- Metrics and trends section (MT Blue)
- 2% boundary from viewport edges
- No overlapping notes for clarity
- Efficient space usage with appropriate note sizes

**Use Case:** Sprint retrospectives, project post-mortems, continuous improvement sessions, and team development.
